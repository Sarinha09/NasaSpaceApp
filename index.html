The Intelligence Behind AstroBit AI

Our model was trained on thousands of data points from Kepler, the K2 mission, and TESS (TOI) to help classify exoplanet candidates.

Random Forest Classifier

We chose Random Forest for its robustness, ability to handle complex data, and for helping identify which features have the greatest impact on classification, which is important for scientific analysis.

Model Workflow

  1. 1
    Data Collection and Preparation

    We used public data from missions such as Kepler, K2, and TESS, handling missing values and normalizing features before training.

  2. 2
    Training and Validation

    The model was trained on 75% of the data and validated on the remaining 25%, using the Cross-Validation technique.

  3. 3
    Deployment

    The finalized model was integrated into the platform to provide real-time classifications.

Performance Metrics

  • Accuracy: ...%

    Percentage of correct predictions made by the model.

  • Precision (Average): ...%

    Of the predicted "planets," how many were actually planets.

  • Recall (Average): ...%

    Of all the real planets, how many did the model detect.

Confusion Matrix

A visualization of the model's performance. A strong main diagonal indicates high accuracy, showing that predictions align with reality.

Feature Importance

Shows which data points were most influential in the model’s decisions. The larger the bar, the more important the feature.
